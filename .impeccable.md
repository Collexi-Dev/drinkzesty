## Design Context

### Users
Women aged 38-55 in perimenopause or menopause. Mix of health-savvy and complete supplement newcomers. They discover zesty through social media, search, and word of mouth equally. Mobile and desktop both matter. They're dealing with brain fog, fatigue, and muscle loss, and looking for something that actually works without the gym-bro supplement culture.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, grounded, honest.** Science-backed but never clinical or cold. Like a knowledgeable friend who happens to have read the research. Lowercase brand voice throughout. Conversational Flemish Dutch as primary copy language. No forced metaphors, no marketer voice, no translated-English phrasing.

### Emotional Goals
The interface should evoke **confidence** (this is real science, not wellness woo), **reassurance** (this is safe, studied, normal), and **vitality** (energy is coming back). All three, not one at the expense of another.

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm, organic, light. The current palette is the foundation:

- **Brand amber** `#F2A922` (primary, energetic, warm)
- **Background** `#FFFDF7` (warm off-white, not sterile)
- **Text** `#2D2D2D` (soft near-black, approachable)
- **Accent green** `#6B8F5E` (natural, grounded)
- **Sand** `#F5EDE0` (section contrast, warmth)

Light mode is correct for this audience and context (daytime browsing, health decisions, trust-building). The palette should feel like morning light on natural materials, not like a tech product or a pharmacy.

### Typography
- **Quicksand** for body text (rounded, warm, accessible)
- **Playfair Display** for display/headings (editorial authority, serif contrast)
- **Caveat** for handwritten annotations (personal, human touch)

### Design Principles

1. **Trust through transparency.** Every claim links to research. The design should feel open and honest, never hiding behind slick marketing. Source references are a design feature, not a footnote.

2. **Warm science, not cold clinical.** The visual language bridges supplement skepticism with research credibility. Organic shapes, warm colors, and conversational copy sit alongside peer-reviewed citations.

3. **Accessibility as respect.** The audience includes women experiencing cognitive fog. Generous font sizes, strong contrast ratios, clear hierarchy, and unhurried pacing are not nice-to-haves but core to the product promise.

4. **One shot, every morning.** The product is simple. The design should be too. No feature overload, no decision paralysis. Clear paths, obvious actions, calm confidence.

5. **Human over polished.** Handwritten annotations, organic blob shapes, and conversational tone over sterile perfection. The interface should feel like it was made by people who care, not generated by a system.
